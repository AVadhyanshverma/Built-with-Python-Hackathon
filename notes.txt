A raw NASA image is just a grid of colored pixels. 
To figure out how far away a galaxy is, astronomers 
traditionally spend weeks manually extracting the exact 
brightness of every single glowing dot across different invisible 
light filters (ultraviolet, infrared, etc.). 

Instead of feeding my AI raw pixels, we use the image's 
Coordinates (Right Ascension & Declination). 
Every NASA image is essentially a map with GPS coordinates for the sky.
